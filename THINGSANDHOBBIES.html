Things and Hobbies

Computer

One thing that I truly like and treasure the most is my first computer. When I was a child, I did not have many friends and often felt quite lonely. At that time, my parents bought me a computer, and it became the most valuable thing I owned. For me, that computer was not just a machine; it was a window to the world. Through it, I could connect with people online, explore new things, and especially enjoy playing video games. Those games brought me joy and excitement, which helped fill the emptiness I felt from lacking friendships in my childhood. Even though I am no longer using that old computer now, since I have moved on to laptops and more modern devices, I still keep it at home as a precious memory. It reminds me of how it helped me overcome difficult times and how technology can sometimes be a friend when we need it most. To me, the computer is more than an object; it is a symbol of comfort, connection, and a piece of my childhood that I will always cherish.

Basketball

At first, I was not very good, but the more I played, the more I enjoyed it. Basketball gives me a similar rush of dopamine to playing video games, but it also allows me to be physically active.
