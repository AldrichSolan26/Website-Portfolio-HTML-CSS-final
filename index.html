About me

My daily life

I am originally from Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam. My home country is renowned for its rich cultural heritage, picturesque landscapes, and diverse culinary traditions.

I consider every day an opportunity to enrich my life. I enjoy cooking, coding, and watching romantic films in my free time. In addition, I am enthusiastic about playing basketball, which helps me maintain both physical fitness and teamwork skills.

I studied at Foreign Trade University (FTU) for one year before transferring to the University of Technology Sydney (UTS), where I am currently pursuing my degree.

Some fun facts about myself

One of the most interesting and somewhat ironic facts about me is my academic journey back in Vietnam. I once studied in one of the most rigorous and competitive environments designed specifically to train students for the national exams in Math, Physics, and English. The atmosphere was highly disciplined, with long study hours, constant practice tests, and a culture where academic achievement was everything. It was the kind of place where students were pushed to their limits in order to secure a place in top universities. After years of effort and dedication, I succeeded in earning a slot at Foreign Trade University — one of the most prestigious economics institutions in southern Vietnam. Not only that, I was admitted into the elite high-quality Business Administration program, which was considered a significant accomplishment. At that time, it felt like the natural and expected outcome of the environment I had been trained in, and for many people around me, it would have been the final goal. But here comes the twist: despite all that preparation and achievement in economics and business, I made a completely different choice. Instead of pursuing the traditional path, I decided to study abroad in Australia and major in Information Technology. It was a decision that surprised many people, given the years of effort I had invested in another direction, but to me it was both exciting and liberating. Looking back, I find it both amusing and meaningful. All the training and discipline I went through shaped my mindset and resilience, even though I ended up taking a different path. It taught me that life is not always about following the most predictable route. Sometimes, the detour becomes the real journey — and in my case, choosing IT in Australia is a story that always makes people smile when they hear how unexpectedly it all turned out.
